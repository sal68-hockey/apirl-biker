The 1950s: Origins & Identity

The 1950s were the formative years of the Hells Angels, a time when they crafted their identity as a rebellious group. Their motorcycles became a symbol of freedom, and they positioned themselves in opposition to the constraints of American society, standing as an anti-authority symbol.

The first chapters formed in California, and their influence started to spread throughout the United States. This was the decade that set the stage for their future dominance in the motorcycle scene.

The 1960s: Expansion & Infamy

During the 1960s, the Hells Angels rose to national infamy. They became the face of rebellious counterculture. By 1969, their involvement in the Altamont Free Concert solidified their infamous reputation.

By the late 60s, their brand of outlaw culture had a massive influence on the hippie movement, even though the two groups clashed ideologically. Their reach extended beyond California, marking them as one of the most recognizable motorcycle clubs in the world.
