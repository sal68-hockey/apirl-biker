The 1950s: Origins & Identity

The Hells Angels Motorcycle Club (HAMC) was founded in 1948 in Fontana, California. During the 1950s, the club expanded throughout California, establishing a reputation for defiance and camaraderie.

Their rugged image and tight brotherhood formed the roots of outlaw biker culture, often clashing with authorities and gaining widespread media attention.

The 1960s: Expansion & Infamy

In the 1960s, the Hells Angels became a global presence. With new chapters opening internationally, their notoriety surged alongside the growing counterculture of the decade.

They became symbols of rebellion, culminating in events like the infamous Altamont Free Concert in 1969, where chaos and tragedy followed their role as security.
